FAQ

Why are you running for City Commission?

I am running for a seat on the Great Falls City Commission to continue my work in building the economy, workforce, education, and community. I firmly believe Great Falls is an excellent place to do business, receive an education, raise a family, and recreate. Great Falls has it.

What are your top priorities if elected?

One of my priorities, if elected, is to encourage collaborative and inclusive partnerships across all the community entities. We are Better Together. 

I am proud to see the impact of partnerships at the local, state and federal level in securing $4.25 million to build the new dental education building at Great Falls College MSU, another $1.5 million economic development grant to expand welding facilities at the college and a $25 million RevUp Montana grant to reduce the skill gap for jobs across Montana and increase work-based learning and apprenticeships. Together, we can adapt and grow as a community.

Why should people vote for you?

I am an experienced leader and have managed multi-million dollar budgets, made difficult decisions to balance revenues with expenditures, and am forward looking and leading. At each college and university where I worked, I created and was recognized for many workforce programs to advance opportunities for others to have better jobs and careers as well as enhance economic development. I led the Training and Economic Development Center at one of the colleges where I worked.

Being adaptable is key to building a future. Our agriculture industry and medical communities are shining examples of having to innovate, take risks, and keep moving forward.   I am a strong supporter of our military partners, having participated in the ESGR program and attended the US Air Force War College’s National Security Forum. My father was a pilot in the 7 th Ferrying Command at Gore Field during WWII having completed his last 18 months of service flying the Burma Hump.
