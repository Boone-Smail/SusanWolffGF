About Susan

Susan is a 5th generation Montanan having grown up on the family ranch in Hall, Montana. 

Susan recently retired from a 45-year career of leading institutions of higher education, most recently as the CEO/Dean of Great Falls College MSU. She has experience working with the Office of the Governor, Montana Legislature and the Montana Congressional delegation.

She has served on numerous local and state boards including the Great Falls Area Chamber of Commerce (serving on the Agriculture and Military Affairs Committees, the Great Falls Development Authority, United Way of Cascade County, the Montana Manufacturing Extension Center, Montana Chamber of Commerce, the Governor’s Work-Based Learning Task Force, the Montana Ambassadors, and is a Rotarian. 

Susan developed the Leadership Corvallis, OR program as a member of their Chamber of Commerce, graduated from that program and also graduated from the Leadership Great Falls program her first year in the community. She is also a 2019 Leadership Montana graduate. She led Great Falls MSU for 9 years, oversaw an $11 million annual budget, and raised private funds for scholarships and the new dental building.

Susan is recognized for creating strong, collaborative relationships with community agencies, business and industry, education partners, and government for workforce, economic, and community development. She was awarded the Michael Malone Educator of the Year in 2015 by the Montana Ambassadors. Susan is a strong advocate for career and technical education, work-place learning, and meeting employer and community needs.
